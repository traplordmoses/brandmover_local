BACKGROUND:
- Midnight #0e0f2b — primary canvas (ALWAYS)
- Ocean #0b2e4e — secondary depth layer for atmospheric perspective

- NEVER white, gray, or warm-toned backgrounds

COLOR RULES:
- Aqua #72e1ff is the dominant glow in every render
- Pink #ffb3d9 appears as warm contrast, never dominant
- Gradients refract through glass surfaces, not painted on
- Colors emit light — they glow, they don't just sit there
- Shadows are deep midnight blue, never gray or brown

If colors read flat, desaturated, or gray → INVALID render.

AURORA GLASS

LIGHTING SYSTEM (LOCKED)

Primary light: NEON RIM LIGHT
- Color: Aqua #72e1ff
- Position: 135° behind-and-above (rim/hair light position)
- Intensity: Strong enough to create visible rim on all edges
- Creates the signature "glowing edge" look

Secondary light: WARM CONTRAST
- Color: Candy Pink #ffb3d9
- Position: 45° front-left (fill light position)
- Intensity: Subtle — 30% of primary. Warmth, not dominance.
- Adds dimensional color contrast to shadows

Ambient: MIDNIGHT ATMOSPHERE
- Color: Deep Ocean #0b2e4e at 15% intensity
- Provides subtle shadow fill — no pure black shadows
- Shadows read as deep midnight blue, never gray

Volumetric: ATMOSPHERIC GLOW
- Subtle volumetric haze from neon sources
- Light scatters through atmosphere like underwater neon
- NOT fog — it's atmospheric light diffusion
- Intensity: visible but not obscuring detail

ENVIRONMENT LIGHT:
- Subtle HDRI reflection showing neon streaks
- Visible in chrome and glass surfaces only
- No visible environment geometry — just color reflections

LIGHTING RULES:
- EVERY object has visible aqua rim lighting. No exceptions.
- Shadows are midnight blue, never gray, never brown
- Light EMITS from neon elements (self-illuminating)
- Volumetric glow is present but never obscures the subject
- No flat studio lighting. No daylight. No warm incandescent.

If rim light is missing or shadows are gray → INVALID render.

====================================================================

GLASS & TRANSPARENCY RULES (CRITICAL)

Glass in the FOID universe is a hero material. It must look premium.

Requirements:
- Optically clean — high clarity, sharp internal details visible
- Visible refraction — light bends through glass realistically
- Colored light bleeding through edges (aqua or pink tint)
- Caustic patterns cast on nearby surfaces from light passing through
- Subtle internal glow when lit from behind
- Fresnel effect: more reflective at grazing angles, more transparent straight-on

NEVER:
- Dirty, frosted, or hazed glass (unless explicitly frosted glass)
- Flat transparent surfaces with no refraction
- Glass that looks like plastic wrap
- Glass without any neon light interaction

EDGE TREATMENT (LOCKED)

- Smooth, rounded edges on ALL objects — the Frutiger Aero signature
- Gentle fillets (1-3mm at render scale)
- No razor-sharp CAD edges
- No exaggerated bevels
- Edges catch rim light as bright highlights
- Overall feel: premium consumer electronics, not industrial machinery

====================================================================

SHARPNESS & FOCUS

- Subject perfectly in focus with crisp edges
- Slight depth-of-field blur on distant background elements ONLY
- No camera blur on the main subject
- No motion blur
- Bloom is ALLOWED but subtle — neon sources can softly bloom
- Crisp material details at all scales

====================================================================

FRAMING & CAMERA

- Three-quarter angle (approximately 30-45° from front)
- Slight low angle for heroic presence (5-10° upward tilt)
- Object fills 60-70% of frame — enough space for glow and atmosphere
- Generous margins for neon halos and volumetric light
- Camera focal length: 85-135mm equivalent (slight telephoto compression)

====================================================================

COMPOSITION RULES

- Subject centered or rule-of-thirds positioned
- Floating objects preferred — hovering slightly above surface
- If platform/surface present: reflective midnight surface showing neon reflections
- Particle effects: subtle floating light particles (aqua) in atmosphere
- Depth layers: foreground blur element (optional), sharp subject, atmospheric background
